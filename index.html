Using Language Model to Bootstrap Human Activity Recognition Ambient Sensors Based in Smart Homes

Abstract

Long Short Term Memory (LSTM)-based structures have demonstrated their efficiency for daily living recognition activities in smart homes by capturing the order of sensor activations and their temporal dependencies. Nevertheless, they still fail in dealing with the semantics and the context of the sensors. More than isolated id and their ordered activation values, sensors also carry meaning. Indeed, their nature and type of activation can translate various activities. Their logs are correlated with each other, creating a global context. We propose to use and compare two Natural Language Processing embedding methods to enhance LSTM-based structures in activity-sequences classification tasks: Word2Vec, a static semantic embedding, and ELMo, a contextualized embedding. Results, on real smart homes datasets, indicate that this approach provides useful information, such as a sensor organization map, and makes less confusion between daily activity classes. It helps to better perform on datasets with competing activities of other residents or pets. Our tests show also that the embeddings can be pretrained on different datasets than the target one, enabling transfer learning. We thus demonstrate that taking into account the context of the sensors and their semantics increases the classification performances and enables transfer learning.

Approach

The proposed approach leverages advanced language models to enhance the contextual representation of IoT activations, crucial for human activity recognition in smart homes. By encoding sparse and unevenly spaced time series data, our method captures semantic and contextual information, smoothing out variability and noise. This involves segmenting sensor data into event sequences, transforming these sequences into categorical values, and pre-training embeddings like ELMo and Word2Vec from scratch over IoT activations. Once pre-trained, these embeddings are frozen and used by a bidirectional LSTM classifier to accurately label activities.

Results

In this study, we explored the unsupervised learning capabilities of Word2Vec and ELMo embeddings for human activity recognition in smart homes. By pre-training these embeddings from scratch on IoT activations, we demonstrated that ELMo, combined with a bidirectional LSTM classifier, significantly outperforms other methods, including Word2Vec and the previous state-of-the-art model by Liciotti et al. ELMo's context-aware embedding not only improved classification accuracy but also facilitated the discovery of new activity patterns. Transfer learning experiments further highlighted ELMo's robustness, showing that an ELMo model trained on one dataset (Aruba) effectively generalized to another (Cairo), maintaining high performance despite different sensor setups. These results underscore the importance of contextual embeddings in enhancing HAR in diverse and dynamic smart home environments.
